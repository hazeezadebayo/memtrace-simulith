CHOOSE YOUR LENS

Each mode is a different lens on uncertainty. Pick the one that fits your question, or use Router for automatic mode selection.

01
⚖️
COUNCIL

Stakeholder Deliberation

Subject your decision to a panel of dynamically generated persona agents who debate, cross-examine, and score strategic branches (Aggressive, Defensive, Lateral). Get a mathematical confidence rating for each path. Use it when you need to stress-test a high-stakes decision against every possible objection.

02
🕸️
MESH

Social Belief Simulation

Spawn up to 30 autonomous agents that form factions, react to market/geopolitical shocks, and drift in beliefs across simulated social platforms. Watch narratives evolve, factions tip, and defections happen in real time. Use it to model how an announcement, policy, or crisis will spread through public discourse.

03
🌳
TREE

Consequence State-Space Search

An MCTS-inspired engine that maps every decision into a branching tree of causally linked outcomes. Each branch is scored by deterministic physics (elasticity, shocks, coupling) and stochastic variance. Use it to find the path with the best expected utility and the worst-case risk — before you move.

HOW IT WORKS

FROM INPUT TO INSIGHT

Bring your context. Get a simulation report.

1. INPUT: Paste text, upload a document, or drop a video/audio/image file
2. PARSE & CLASSIFY: Content is chunked, embedded, and domain-matched
3. SIMULATE: Agents spawn, interact, and evolve across configurable rounds
4. REPORT: Quantitative scores, narrative summary, and confidence index
MEDIA INPUT: Upload a video, audio recording, or image — the engine extracts transcripts and visual context to simulate reactions to your content.

ORCHESTRATION LAYER

Router & Divergence — two independent paths.

SYNTHESIZED DATA

🤖 ROUTER

LLM analyzes intent and auto-dispatches to the optimal mode (Council, Mesh, or Tree)

🔀 DIVERGENCE

Runs all three modes simultaneously on the same question and compares outputs

📋 SYNTHESIS & REPORT

Cross-mode confidence scoring with disagreement highlighted — divergence is the signal

TWO PATHS IN: Router auto-selects the best-fit mode from your intent. Divergence runs all three and surfaces where they conflict. Same question, two valid entries — one synthesized answer.

WHAT YOU GET BACK

Actionable intelligence, not just logs.

📊 CONFIDENCE INDEX: Mathematical score of outcome likelihood
⚡ DOMINANT RISK: The single most likely failure mode
👥 KEY ACTORS: Who drives the narrative and whose stance shifts fastest
💬 POST-SIM INTERVIEWS: Chat directly with any agent to probe its reasoning
FULL TRACEABILITY: Every confidence score, risk label, and actor ranking is grounded in agent-level reasoning. Click any output to interview the agent behind it — no black boxes.

WHY SIMULITH

THREE LENSES + TWO ORCHESTRATORS

Council (deliberation), Mesh (social dynamics), Tree (causal search) — each reveals different failure modes. Router auto-selects the right one. Divergence runs all three and surfaces where they disagree. Together they expose what a single answer hides.

UNCERTAINTY IS THE OUTPUT

Most AI collapses ambiguity into one confident answer. Simulith preserves and quantifies disagreement. If Council says "60% Defensive wins" but Mesh shows a faction cascade against it, that divergence is the intelligence — not a bug.

LOCAL-FIRST & PRIVATE

All context extraction, embedding, and simulation logic runs locally or on your infrastructure. No data leaves your environment unless you choose cloud inference.

PERSISTENT MEMORY SUBSTRATE

Every simulation outcome is ingested back into your knowledge graph. Past forecasts become context for future queries. You build a living library of tested assumptions over time — not throwaway answers.

MEDIA-AWARE INPUTS

Go beyond text. Upload video, audio, or images — the engine extracts transcripts, visual context, and metadata to simulate how audiences interpret your actual content, not just your summary of it.

REPORTERAGENT INTERVIEWS

Post-simulation, you can chat directly with any agent to cross-examine its reasoning. "Why did you defect in Round 2?" — and get a structured answer with belief deltas. No black boxes.
